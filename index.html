Ronin Toys

Current Work (September 2025–Present)

I run Ronin Toys, where I design and manufacture cat toys. Right now that's the Reelin (a fishing-rod style toy) and Rattletail (a chaseable toy for cats that doubles up as a fidget toy for humans), both 3D-printed in PLA+ plastic. They're durable, they're fun, and cats lose their minds over them in the best possible way.

At Byju's, I designed learning modules for 3-5 year olds—the whole brief was "make education feel like play." Turns out, designing play experiences for cats uses a lot of the same thinking: what holds attention, what creates joy, what makes you want to do it again.

Why This Works Right Now

India's toy market has opened up in interesting ways. There's space for small-batch, thoughtfully designed products that aren't just trying to be cheaper than everything else. Pet parents are investing in quality. And manufacturing—whether it's 3D printing, woodworking, or textile production—is more accessible than it's been before.

I'm building something that's economically sustainable and built to last.

The Vision (And Why I'm Talking About It)

The Reelin is moving to wood—properly finished, properly made, the kind of object that lasts and doesn't end up in a landfill. Getting there means building a network of artisans: woodworkers and carpenters who know their craft and want to make things that matter.

I'm working on building that network now. The model I'm aiming for puts at least 30% of profits directly to the artisans—as fair payment for skilled work.

So, that's the direction as I know it. I'm figuring it out as I go, which honestly makes it more interesting.

How I'm Building This

Right now, everything is self-funded and friend-powered. I've been learning 3D printing with support from Kailash at Ampere Works, and other friends have helped with web design, marketing, and more. The overhead is low and I'm doing as much as I can myself.

What Would Help

I'm running this myself, and there are specific things that would help this grow in the right direction:

  • Funding — Trusts, NGOs, grant programs, or people interested in supporting ethical manufacturing and maker networks. I'm not looking for equity investment; I'm looking for funding that understands infrastructure-building and public good. This is about building artisan networks and transitioning to sustainable materials, not about exits or scale.
  • Connections — Artisans working with wood or upcycled fabric. People who've built artisan networks before and can share what actually works. Anyone who knows how to structure small-batch manufacturing sustainably. I need practical knowledge from people who've done this, not just theories about how it might work.
  • Orders — If you have cats (or know people who do), the toys are here. Word of mouth goes a long way. Every order helps prove the model, refine the products, and build toward the wood transition.

If any of that resonates with you—or if you just want to talk about what I'm building—reach out at ruphilka@ronintoys.com. I'm always up for a good conversation.
