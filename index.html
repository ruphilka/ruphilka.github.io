Ronin Toys

Current Work (September 2025–Present)

I run Ronin Toys, where I design and manufacture cat toys. They're durable, they're fun, and cats lose their minds over them in the best possible way. Right now the toys are just 3D-printed, PLA+ plastic.

At Byju's, the whole brief was "make education feel like play." Turns out, designing play experiences for cats uses a lot of the same thinking: what holds attention, what instigates feline play, what makes you the human, want to engage again.

Why This Works Right Now

India's toy market has opened up in interesting ways. There's space for small-batch, thoughtfully designed products. Pet parents are investing in quality. And manufacturing, whether it's 3D printing, woodworking, or textile production—is more accessible than it's been before.

I'm building something that's economically sustainable and built to last.

The Vision (And Why I'm Talking About It)

The Reelin is moving to wood. Properly finished, properly made, the kind of object that lasts and doesn't end up in a landfill. Getting there means building a network of artisans: woodworkers and carpenters who know their craft and want to make things that matter.

I'm working on building that network now. The model I'm aiming for puts at least 30% of profits directly to the artisans, as fair payment for skilled work.

So, that's the direction as I know it. I'm figuring it out as I go, which honestly makes it more interesting.

How I'm Building This

Right now, everything is self-funded and friend-powered. I've been learning 3D printing with support from Kailash at Ampere Works, and other friends have helped with web design, marketing, and more. The overhead is low and I'm doing as much as I can myself.

What Would Help

  • Funding — Trusts, NGOs, grant programs, or people interested in supporting ethical manufacturing and maker networks. I'd like help building artisan networks and transitioning to sustainable materials.
  • Connections — Artisans working with wood or upcycled fabric. I'd love to get practical knowledge from people who've built artisan networks before and can share what actually works, and anyone who knows the ins and outs of manufacturing sustainably.
  • Orders — If you have cats (or know people who do), the toys are here. Word of mouth goes a long way.

If you want to talk about what I'm building, feel free to reach out at ruphilka@ronintoys.com. I'm always up for a good conversation.
